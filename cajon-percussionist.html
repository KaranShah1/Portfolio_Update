About the Cajon

The cajon (pronounced "ka-HONE") is a Peruvian percussion instrument that has gained popularity worldwide. It's essentially a wooden box that you sit on while playing, producing a variety of sounds through different striking techniques.

Technical Skills

Playing Techniques

  • Bass Tones: Deep, resonant sounds produced by striking the center of the playing surface
  • Treble Tones: Higher, snappier sounds created by hitting the edges and corners
  • Slap Techniques: Sharp, percussive sounds using fingertips and palms
  • Brush Techniques: Subtle, textured sounds using brushes or hands
  • Polyrhythmic Patterns: Complex rhythmic combinations and cross-rhythms
